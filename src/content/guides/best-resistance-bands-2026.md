## How to Choose Resistance Bands

**Loop bands and tube bands with handles serve different purposes.** Flat loop bands excel at lower-body work like glute bridges, lateral walks, and mobility exercises, while tube bands with handles and a door anchor open up full-body exercises that mimic cable machine movements — rows, presses, and curls.

**Pull-up assistance bands are a distinct category worth buying separately.** These heavier-duty bands are specifically designed to support body weight during pull-up progressions, and using lighter general-purpose bands for this purpose won't provide adequate support or durability.

**Latex-free options matter for sensitive skin or allergies.** If you've had reactions to latex bands in the past, look specifically for latex-free labeling — options like fabric-wrapped or specific synthetic materials avoid this issue entirely.

**A full set with multiple resistance levels offers the most training longevity.** Starting with a single resistance level means outgrowing the band relatively quickly as you get stronger — sets with 5 or more graduated levels let you progress without repurchasing.
