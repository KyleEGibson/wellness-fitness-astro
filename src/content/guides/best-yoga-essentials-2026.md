## How to Choose Yoga Equipment

**Mat thickness is a genuine tradeoff, not just preference.** Thicker mats (1/2 inch or more) provide more joint cushioning, which matters if you have sensitive knees or wrists, but thinner mats offer better stability and ground connection for balance-focused poses. If you're unsure, a moderate thickness around 1/4 to 1/2 inch suits most practices.

**Grip matters more than most beginners realize.** A mat that slides during a sweaty flow class undermines confidence and safety in poses. Look for reviews specifically mentioning grip performance when hands or feet get sweaty, not just initial out-of-box texture.

**Blocks aren't just for beginners.** Even experienced practitioners use blocks regularly to deepen stretches, modify poses for injuries, or add stability in balance poses — they're a genuinely useful long-term prop rather than a training-wheels item you'll outgrow.

**EVA foam vs. cork or wood blocks is a weight and feel preference.** EVA foam blocks are lighter and softer, making them comfortable for seated support, while cork or wood options offer more stability for weight-bearing use, though they're heavier to hold during a flow.
